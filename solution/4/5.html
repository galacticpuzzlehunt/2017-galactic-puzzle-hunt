Solution: Stephen's Speed Run

Written by Nathan Pinsker

The puzzle consists of a short snippet of flavor text and a link to a PuzzleScript game. The text refers to someone named Stephen and his "favorite food", which is meant to steer solvers towards determining what these things are, as well as how the game might work. Searching phrases like "stephen's speed run" or "stephen game" on Google yields a result for "Stephen's Sausage Roll", a 2016 puzzle game, and solvers will notice that the mechanics of that game exactly match those of the PuzzleScript game provided.

The flavor text also says Stephen "wants to see how fast" he can cook the sausages, which translates to a goal of determining the minimal number of moves required to complete each level. These minimal numbers, along with one possible solution for each level, are provided below. (All levels require only the use of the arrow keys, denoted as U, D, L, and R for up, down, left and right respectively.)

  • Level 1: 25 (LDRLD LRDRU UULDD DRLLL ULRRD)
  • Level 2: 34 (UDLLD DDDRR RRDUU RUURL LUULU DLLDR RUUR)
  • Level 3: 54 (UUULR DULRR URRRU DRDLU ULLLL LUDRD LLDDD DDRRR RRUUL DDLLL LLDU)
  • Level 4: 48 (LDDRL DLLDU LURDD RRRDU RRUUU LDDDR LDDLL URRUU LRDUR UDL)
  • Level 5: 18 (DRDLL ULRDU RURLU LRU)
  • Level 6: 30 (UULUD DRLDD LLURR UURRR DURUR LULUD)
  • Level 7: 30 (DULLU URUDL DRRRD LLUUR DDRRD DLLLR)
  • Level 8: 34 (RULDL RRURR RUDRD DLUUU RLLLU LLLDL URDR)
  • Level 9: 26 (DUDLL URRRU UURLU LLUDD LDRRU L)

At any point during this process, solvers may try to poke around for additional sources of data. There are two additional links on the PuzzleScript game's page; one of these leads to the PuzzleScript homepage, and the other opens an editor containing the game's source code. At the top of the source code is a comment: "Stephen wants to see how the sausage is made." which is meant to encourage solvers that they're on the right track. Scrolling down reveals that each puzzle is hiding an invisible message starting with "+" and then a moderately-sized two-digit number. This suggests that the minimal number of moves in each level should be added to the corresponding number provided here to obtain a new number associated with each level:

52 50 84 82 73 76 79 71 89

The range of numbers obtained, along with the flavor text mentioning "character" in the source code, suggest that converting each number to its ASCII character might be a good option. Doing so yields the characters

4 2 T R I L O G Y

cluing THE HITCHHIKER'S GUIDE TO THE GALAXY, the correct answer.

Author's Notes

Solvers may notice that all the levels are actually levels included in Stephen's Sausage Roll itself! I chose to include these levels simply because they facilitated interesting gameplay far better than anything I could come up with myself. Since the game imposes no ordering on these levels, the only information solvers could obtain from noticing this was the name of each level, which I hoped would be insubstantial enough not to steer solvers too far from the correct path.

The "move counter" at the top-right of each level was added only 12 hours before this puzzle went live. We received a lot of feedback about how it wasn't clear enough when you had an optimal solution, and I don't want to imagine what it would have been like without that feature!

The mechanics of this puzzle ended up coming together in a way that I'm not entirely happy with. The current cluephrase is a little abstruse, and I would have liked to give a longer and more descriptive one. On the other hand, I was worried that doing so would make the puzzle more difficult and time-consuming than it already was, especially for those who hadn't played Stephen's Sausage Roll. Pushing people to find optimal solutions can be tricky -- merely asking for the length of the solution, or informing solvers when they've found the optimal solution, can be taken advantage of by some clever guessing. That said, I think this puzzle was able to differentiate itself as one where it's obvious what to do, but actually doing it is the hard part. I hope that some teams really enjoyed the process of improving their skills and mastering the mechanics of the game.
