Solution: Unaligned

Written by Colin Lu, Jon Schneider, and Anderson Wang, based on an idea by Rahul Sridhar and Patrick Xia

The gimmick of this crossword puzzle is that all sets of clues are “unaligned”: that is, the 2–5 clues in each set may be in the wrong order and might need to be “rotated” before entering them into the grid, using crossing letters to determine how much to rotate. For example, the 1-across answers are DISNEY, GABLE, and MOAN, but they are entered into the grid as GABLEMOANDISNEY. The 5-digit binary sequences for grid 1 are 00011, 01011, 10011, 01100, 01111, which convert to 3, 11, 19, 12, 15, which is CKSLO alphanumerically. For grid 2, the binary sequences are 01111, 10010, 01110, 10011, and 01000, which convert to 15, 18, 14, 19, 8, or ORNSH.

By this point, we’re sick of rotating words around, but we still need to do it two more times to get the final answer: LOCKS HORNS. Here, the word LOCKS is supposed to evoke locks where you rotate dials with letters on them, which is what we’ve been doing throughout the puzzle.

Author's Notes

This puzzle evolved from a puzzle which consisted of 100 crossword clues for 3-letter words which, like in this puzzle, could be rotated to spell out 5-letter words down the middle. Needless to say, solving 100 clues, most of which are standard crosswordese or worse, is not very fun, and the extraction wasn’t that interesting either.

When ordering the 5-letter words, it’s possible that some teams might try the reverse ordering at first (e.g. CLOSE, MINOR, STORE, OFFER, SEIZE instead of CLOSE, SEIZE, OFFER, STORE, MINOR) because there isn’t a canonical direction of rotation. The reason we chose this direction is that the nth 5-letter word starts in the nth row after rotating, which feels more natural for an ordering. In any case, we hope that the LEFT IS ZERO/RIGHT IS ONE messages will be eventually found and confirm the correct ordering.
