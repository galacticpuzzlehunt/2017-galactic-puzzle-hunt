Solution: Conference 2

Written by Jon Schneider and Anderson Wang

Each of these materials, when processed, gives a word that can fill in the blanks on the right side. For example, TOMATO goes to KETCHUP, which corresponds to the first line. However, neither TOMATO nor SEMIAUTOMATON fits on the left side, which has 8 blanks. The realization here is that the answers do fit in the left side blanks if the hidden word is compressed into a single blank, such as SEMIAU(TOMATO)N having length 8.

Some of the processed materials on the right side might not be completely unambiguous (e.g. CLAY could also go to POTTERY, or LIME could go to CONCRETE), but combined with the first letter and length constraints, there is only one way to match everything up.

Finally, we index the position of the left side blank containing the secret ingredient into the right side, which gives the answer PULP FICTION.

Pulp is an ingredient that is processed into paper, so this meta can be associated with the PAPER symbol in Rock Paper Scissors (indeed, paper itself is a "pulp fiction").

Author's Notes

Of the three metas, we expected this one to be solved first, as the hidden words (especially the food-related ones like WHEAT and MILK) pretty quickly jumped out at solvers during testsolving.
